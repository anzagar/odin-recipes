Pernil

Description

Pernil is Puerto Rico’s iconic roasted pork shoulder, especially popular during Christmas and other celebrations. The dish is marinated for hours (or even overnight) in a garlicky, citrusy seasoning blend called adobo mojado, which infuses the meat with deep flavor. The pork is then slow-roasted until the meat becomes tender and juicy, while the skin crisps up into irresistible chicharrón.

Pernil is often served alongside rice dishes like arroz con gandules, making it the centerpiece of Puerto Rican holiday feasts. The contrast of crispy skin, savory pork, and seasoned rice is considered one of the most traditional and beloved combinations in the cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole pork shoulder (pernil), 6–8 lbs
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp vinegar or sour orange juice
  • 1 tbsp oregano (dried)
  • 1 tbsp adobo seasoning (or salt and pepper to taste)
  • 1 tsp sazón (optional, for color)

Steps

  1. Prepare the marinade by mixing garlic, olive oil, vinegar (or sour orange juice), oregano, adobo, and sazón.
  2. Score the pork shoulder’s skin and make small slits in the meat. Rub the marinade all over, pushing seasoning into the cuts.
  3. Marinate the pork in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, for maximum flavor.
  4. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Place the pork shoulder in a roasting pan, skin side up.
  5. Roast uncovered for 4–5 hours, basting occasionally with its juices. The skin should become crispy and the meat tender.
  6. Increase oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) for the last 20–30 minutes to crisp up the skin further.
  7. Remove from oven, let rest for 15 minutes, then carve and serve with arroz con gandules or mofongo.
