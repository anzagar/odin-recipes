Pastelón

Description

Pastelón is often described as a Puerto Rican or Dominican lasagna but instead of pasta sheets, it uses sweet ripe plantains layered with seasoned ground beef, cheese, and sometimes vegetables. The combination of sweet plantains with savory, garlicky meat makes it a festive comfort dish, perfect for special occasions.

This dish highlights how important plantains are to the Caribbean Cuisine. Just like with Mofongo you have a dish where Plantain is the centerpiece.

Ingredients

  • 4–5 ripe plantains (yellow with black spots), sliced lengthwise and fried.
  • 1 lb ground beef or pork
  • ½ cup sofrito
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • ½ cup shredded mozzarella or cheddar
  • 2 eggs, beaten (for binding)
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

Steps

  1. Fry sliced plantains until golden and tender; set aside.
  2. In a pan, cook sofrito with tomato sauce, then add ground meat and season well (salt, pepper, oregano, adobo). Cook until browned.
  3. In a baking dish, layer fried plantains, then meat, then cheese. Repeat layers, finishing with plantains on top.
  4. Pour beaten eggs over the dish to set. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 30 minutes until firm and golden.
  5. Let cool slightly before slicing.
